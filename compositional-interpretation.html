Compositional Interpretation

The Ross way of seeing systems as compositions, not fragments.

Definition

Compositional Interpretation is the ability to understand, translate and align multiple components of a system — technical, creative, operational, legal and human — by seeing how they fit together as a coherent whole.

It is not about playing every instrument. It is about understanding the composition and interpreting the score.

How Ross Uses It

Ross Consultancy reads code, contracts, workflows, designs and user journeys as parts of a single structure. We interpret how they interact, where they clash, and how they can be brought into alignment.

This perspective underpins our Six‑Point Multi‑Modality System and Six‑Layer Outcome Model.
